Transistor Operation

The emitter-base junction is forward biased.
The bas-collector junction is reverse biased.

Transistor Currents

Region of Operations

Active region:

In the active region the collector-base junction is reverse-biased, while the base-emitter junction is forward-biased.

Cut-off region:

In the cutoff region the collector-base and base emitter junctions of a transistor are both reverse biased.

Saturation region:

In the saturation region the collector-base and base emitter junctions are forward-biased.

Breakdown region:

In this region the reverse bias of CB junction is increased to such an extent that the junction breaks down.

Early effect

By increasing the voltage at the collector it is possible to increase the collector current. This is because on increasing the reverse biased collector voltage, the base width decreases since the collector depletion region penetrates more into the base region . This phenomenon is called as Early effect , named after its discoverer.
