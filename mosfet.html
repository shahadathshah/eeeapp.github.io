Field Effect Transistor

The field-effect transistor (FET) is a type of transistor which uses an electric field to control the flow of current. FETs are devices with three terminals: source, gate, and drain.

Depletion-Type MOSFET

There is no direct electrical connection between the gate terminal and the channel of a MOSFET. \nIt is the insulating layer of SiO2 in the MOSFET construction that accounts for the very desirable high input impedance of the device. Because of the very high input impedance, the gate current IG is essentially 0 A for dc biased configurations
